Section A
Macro India Context
Indian Economy · Per Capita Income · Demographics · Urbanisation · Growth Drivers
Why this matters for apparel: India is the fastest-growing major economy globally, projected to be a USD 6.5T economy by FY29. A young, urbanising, increasingly affluent population with rising women workforce participation creates a structurally supportive backdrop for organised apparel retail — particularly South India where incomes and ethnic wear affinity are both high.
A.1 — Indian Economy: GDP & Growth

India has consistently outpaced major global economies in GDP growth, peaking at 18.9% in FY2022 post-COVID. Despite a brief contraction in FY2021 (-1.2%), the economy rebounded sharply. India is ranked 5th globally by nominal GDP (FY2024) and is projected to become 3rd largest by FY2029, surpassing Germany and Japan.

THESIS
Macro tailwind: India growing at 2x the world average GDP rate since FY2005. Even assuming PFCE share stays flat at ~60% of GDP, the absolute consumption pool will nearly double by FY29 — directly expanding the addressable apparel market without requiring any share gain.
MODEL
Benchmarking: China GDP slowing from 7.8% (2012) to projected 3.3% (2029) vs India sustaining 8–10%. Among India-comparable consumer markets — China 5%, Vietnam 4.8%, USA 1.7%, Germany 0.5% on per capita GNI CAGR 2018–23. India is the fastest income-growth story globally.
A.2 — Per Capita Consumption & Income

India's per capita final consumption expenditure (PFCE) has grown strongly, recovering from the COVID dip. GNI per capita reached INR 208,633 in FY2024 — up ~47.5% from FY2019 (CAGR 8.1%), outpacing USA (4.7%), UK (2.6%), and China (5.0%).

A.3 — Demographics & Age Structure

India became the world's most populous nation in 2023 (~1,430 million). 74% of population is aged 15–45, with median age of just 29.5 years (vs 38.5 USA, 39.8 China). Millennials + Gen Z comprise 50%+ of total population.

A.4 — Urbanisation & Digital Economy

India's urban population stands at 519 million (36%) vs global average of 57%. Projected to reach 613 million (41%) by 2030. Urban population contributes 63% of GDP. Digital India has driven internet users from 350M (2015) to 881M (2024) — CAGR 12%.

Key Risks
RISK
Price sensitivity & consumer value-seeking: Inflation squeezes mid-segment. Growing resale and off-price segments. RSB's multi-format (value + premium) model provides some hedge but mid-premium AOV declining (₹2,834 → ₹2,551 FY23–25) needs monitoring.
RISK
National LFS & D2C competition: Trent (1,043 stores), Reliance (17,000+ stores), ABFRL expanding into South Indian Tier I/II cities. D2C brands capturing younger digital shoppers. RSB's regional depth is its moat but scale gap with national players is widening.
RISK
Online clothing rental market: Platforms like Flyrobe, Rent an Attire growing in occasion-wear (directly competing with RSB's festive/ethnic segment). Currently urban-centric but could spread to Tier II cities over 3–5 years.
