Design decisions worth flagging to your analyst
Each description = the conclusion, not the topic. "Market context" alone tells the reader nothing. "Value fashion is simultaneously the largest, fastest-growing, and least-organised segment" tells them what they should believe before they even read the slide. This is Bernstein style — answer up front.
The thesis line (top right) is optional. If your analyst thinks it's too aggressive for a TOC, remove it — the individual act descriptions carry enough weight. But if you keep it, make it italic and right-aligned exactly as shown, so it reads as an annotation rather than a claim.
